<p align="center"><img class="img-fluid" src="{{ "pic/PIC-BLT/BLT-4144.PUL.jpg" }}" width="600" height="480"></p>
<h3 align="center">Undo this screw</h3>
<p align="center"><img class="img-fluid" src="{{ "pic/PIC-BLT/BLT-4144.11.jpg" }}" width="600" height="480"></p>
<h3 align="center">You will also need to loose the screw which holds the motor in place</h3>
<p align="center">(be careful not to remove it completely as it can be awkward to get back in)</p>
<p align="center"><img class="img-fluid" src="{{ "pic/PIC-BLT/BLT-4144.13.jpg" }}" width="600" height="480"></p>
<h3 align="center">With the belt already around the balance wheel, and in the pulley, <br>
slip the pulley onto the drive shaft and replace and tighten the screw</h3>
<p align="center"><img class="img-fluid" src="{{ "pic/PIC-BLT/BLT-4144.12.jpg" }}" width="600" height="480"></p>
<h3 align="center">And then all there is left to do is push the motor to a position <br>
where the belt is appropriately taught then retighten the screw that holds the motor in place.</h3>
